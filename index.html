                  Jen AggOwner

                  I refuse to speak about myself in the third person, as though I didn't write all these bios, so this is awkward. I like building restaurants, my first, The Black Hoof is still the heartbeat of my company, which mostly takes up a small block on Dundas West in Toronto (along with Rhum Corner and Cocktail Bar). In early 2016, with my husband, artist, Roland Jean and Win Butler & Régine Chassagne (of Arcade Fire) we opened Rhum spin-off restaurant Agrikol in Montreal + a little bar next door, ti agrikol. Also, I wrote a book 'I Hear She's a Real Bitch', so it's been kind of a busy year. But it's most important to note that I am, above all, more fun than at first I might appear. Much more fun. I like wine. No. I love wine.

                  Jake SkakunGeneral Manager & Sommelier

                  When young Jake was working at a Vancouver area Milestones, he rose through the company quickly (THEY KNEW WHAT THEY HAD) and was poised to be on the fast track to management until one day in front of his coworkers, his GM asked him which was fuller-bodied, a cab or a shiraz. Jake faltered, because he didn't really know (turns out his boss didn’t know what he was talking about either), and from that moment, he vowed to learn everything he could about wine. He quit Milestones to manage at Salt Tasting Room, and later spent a year studying and bouncing around San Francisco restaurants, doing stages and picking up whatever knowledge he could. Eventually he landed at Vancouver's much lauded L'Abattoir, and worked while he continued his somm studies and found time to write a blog about all things wine--he had really fallen in love. I have no answer for what I'd do without him, but I'd be in big trouble.

                  David GreigBar Manager

                  After first pulling pints for ex-football hooligans in a North London pub 15 years ago, David started cutting his cocktail teeth in Brighton. A few years later he returned to London to continue training in a city where the standard is world-class (including the bar at All Star Lanes Brick Lane, where post-shift beers were supplemented with a game of bowling, a work perk he is still hoping to re-introduce to his life at some point). By the time he ended up here (via Vancouver where he and Jake began their beautiful bromance at L'Abattoir ) his skills as a bartender were incredibly polished. His curiosity is endless and he knows more about spirits and how to lift them into sums greater than their parts than anyone I've ever met. Without him, Cocktail Bar would not be the vibrant training ground it is, he is truly an inspiring leader, whose infectious enthusiasm is mirrored by his proteges. David also likes the WWF. WTF?

                  James SantonChef

                  James worked at Raw Bar (RIP). Back then he was a much younger cook with maybe a teeny, tiny, little chip on his shoulder (or maybe he just didn’t like me...GET IN LINE BUDDY) anyway I’m almost done making his bio about me. He says he remembers me telling him, very directly, as he occasionally moved with a slight dragging of feet to “walk with purpose”. And that it stuck with him. He went off to be a sous chef, then CDC of Enoteca Sociale where Roland and I often enjoyed his very delicious Italian cooking. Years had passed since Raw Bar and we were very congenial—one time, at the coffee shop, I even stopped a dog thief (can you IMAGINE) from making off with his beautiful boxador—I’m told this is a real, recognized breed—Riley. Shit. I’m still making it about me. James is a brilliant cook. He makes food from instinct that he’s not necessarily that familiar with and NAILS IT. Every time. It’s frankly unsettling. He’s also a lovely and hilarious person who runs his kitchen with the kind of leadership I hope to someday see in all restaurants.
